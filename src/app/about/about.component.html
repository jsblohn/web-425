Johann Strauss II


Johann Strauss II was born in Vienna, Australia on October 25, 1825. His father, Johann Strauss I, was also a composer. Johann II wrote over 500 musical compositions; 150 of them were waltzes. His most famous waltz is probably The Blue Danube, which was written in 1867, and helped to establish him as “the Waltz King”.

In 1844, he began conducting music while he was still a teenager. He began composing music soon after. He quit conducting for the most part around 1862 in order to focus on writing music.

He died on June 3, 1899 from a respiratory illness that turned into pneumonia. Source
