Harper Lee

Harper Lee is best known for her Pulitzer Prize-winning novel To Kill a Mockingbird which was published in 1960. She also wrote a second novel, Go Set a Watchman, which was written before To Kill a Mockingbird, but wasn’t published until 2015. It included the same characters in their later lives.

She was born April 28, 1926, in Monroeville, Alabama. In November of 2007 she was presented with the Presidential Medal of Freedom by President George W. Bush for her “outstanding contribution to America’s literary tradition”. She died at the age of 89 on February 19, 2016.
